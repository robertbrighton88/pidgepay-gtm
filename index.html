What is PidgePay?

The product in plain terms — and why it matters for Web3 adoption.

🔗

Create a payment link

Enter an amount in USDC and an optional description. PidgePay generates a shareable link — no wallet address required from the sender.

📤

Share it anywhere

Drop the link in WhatsApp, Telegram, Discord, X DMs — wherever your group lives. No app install for the recipient.

⚡

Get paid in seconds

Recipient connects their Web3 wallet via RainbowKit, pays in USDC on their chain of choice. Settled instantly, fees under $0.01.

The one-liner

"PidgePay is Tikkie for crypto — a shareable USDC payment request link that works across any chain, with no app and no friction."

Tikkie (Netherlands) processes billions in P2P payments via WhatsApp links. PidgePay brings the same UX pattern to Web3 and stablecoins.

Risks & Mitigations

Web3 GTM has unique failure modes. Know them early.

Wallet friction kills conversion

Most non-crypto users don't have a Web3 wallet. The link → payment funnel breaks if someone can't connect a wallet.

Mitigation: Add fiat on-ramp (Stripe, MoonPay) so recipients can pay by card and receiver gets USDC. Phase 2 priority.

Regulatory uncertainty

USDC payments may trigger MSB (Money Services Business) registration requirements in some jurisdictions.

Mitigation: Legal opinion in target markets before launch. Consider Circle's compliance infrastructure as a starting point.

Small addressable market (today)

Web3-native users are still a niche. Tikkie had millions of ING customers to launch into — PidgePay needs to build that density from scratch.

Mitigation: Partner with a wallet provider (Rainbow, Coinbase Wallet) for embedded distribution — the modern equivalent of the ING partnership.

Chain proliferation complexity

"Any chain" is a UX and engineering challenge. Paying on the wrong chain or needing to bridge adds friction.

Mitigation: Default to Base or Polygon (low fees, USDC native) while supporting others. Use Circle's CCTP for cross-chain settlement.

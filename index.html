Product Positioning

How PidgePay fits alongside the two most successful P2P payment apps in history.

Tikkie

PidgePay

  • Core use case: P2P · split · settle

"No awkward IOUs."

Target Market

Start hyper-local. Both Venmo and Tikkie proved that density beats breadth in early stages.

Primary Segment

  • →Age: 18–35, mobile-first digital natives
  • →Behaviour: Frequent social spending — dining, travel, events, rent splitting
  • →Tech profile: Already uses digital banking; no patience for friction
  • →Pain point: Chasing people for money is awkward and slow

Secondary Segment

  • →Small groups: clubs, flatmates, sports teams
  • →Small businesses collecting informal payments
  • →Freelancers and gig workers requesting payment
Tikkie lesson: Monetise the secondary segment (businesses) while keeping P2P free for primary users.

Geographic Focus Principle

Pick one city or country first. Venmo launched in NYC and spread virally through college networks. Tikkie launched exclusively in the Netherlands via ING Bank distribution. Hyper-local density matters more than broad launch reach.

Competitive Landscape

Every incumbent has a weakness. PidgePay's job is to exploit the gap.

Competitor Weakness PidgePay Can Exploit
Venmo US-only; social feed is controversial privacy-wise
Tikkie Netherlands-only; limited to ABN AMRO/ING ecosystem originally
PayPal Friction-heavy; perceived as commercial, not personal
Cash App US-focused; less social/group-oriented
Bank Transfers Slow, no UX, require full account/IBAN numbers — no social layer

GTM Phases

Four phases from zero to expansion — each with a clear goal and playbook.

0

Pre-Launch

Months 1–2 Goal: Validate PMF
  • · Recruit 200–500 beta users from one dense network (university, company, community)
  • · Define the "aha moment": user receives a payment request and settles in <30 seconds
  • · Instrument analytics around that moment
  • · Establish regulatory/compliance baseline (PSD2, eMoney licence, or partner with licensed entity)
Tikkie parallel: Launched inside ING's app — instant regulatory cover + millions of users. Seek a similar distribution partner (neobank, credit union, employer platform).
1

Seed Launch

Months 3–6 Goal: 10,000 active users

Acquisition

  • · Referral loop — every payment request is an acquisition event
  • · University partnerships — student unions, sports clubs, housing
  • · Event sponsorship — pub quizzes, group dinners, sports leagues
  • · One local tech/finance PR story

Retention

  • · Push notification when someone requests money from you
  • · Weekly summary: "You settled €X with 3 friends"
  • · Group payment / split features
Key metric: Payment request → new user sign-up conversion rate. Target: >25%. (Tikkie achieves ~40% on WhatsApp links.)
2

Growth

Months 7–18 Goal: 100,000 users + monetisation

Channels

  • · Paid social — Instagram, TikTok (18–30 demographic)
  • · WhatsApp/iMessage deep links for shareable payment requests
  • · Bank/fintech partnerships for embedded distribution
  • · Content marketing: "How to split [X] fairly" SEO

Monetisation Tests

  • · Instant settlement fee (Venmo: 1.75%)
  • · Business tier for merchants/freelancers
  • · Premium features (scheduling, group wallets)
  • · Float revenue on balances (regulatory-dependent)
Tikkie lesson: Monetise last, grow first. Tikkie was free for years while building trust, then introduced business tiers.
3

Expansion

Month 18+ Goal: Second geo or vertical
  • · Enter a second city/country only after >60% monthly retention in the first
  • · Consider business payments vertical (freelancers, markets, gig workers)
  • · Explore API/SDK for other apps to embed PidgePay as a payment rail

The Growth Loop

Every transaction is a distribution event. This is how Venmo and Tikkie grew with near-zero paid acquisition in early stages.

Key insight

"The payment request link is the product."

Everything else is infrastructure. Make sharing a request so frictionless that every user becomes a distribution channel automatically.

Regulatory & Trust Checklist

Non-negotiables before public launch. Get these wrong and you cannot operate.

eMoney licence or Payment Institution licence

Jurisdiction-specific — essential before handling real money

PSD2 / Open Banking (EU) or FinCEN (US)

Depends on target geography

KYC / AML for onboarding

Know Your Customer checks required from day one

Fraud detection from day one

Friendly fraud is the #1 P2P risk

Clear dispute / chargeback policy

Published before launch — sets user expectations

GDPR / data residency compliance

Privacy controls built correctly from the start

Venmo warning: Venmo was fined $40M by the FTC in 2022 for misleading users about privacy settings. Build privacy controls correctly from the start — not as an afterthought.

Open Questions

Resolve these before committing to a GTM timeline.

  1. 1

    What geography is PidgePay targeting?

    This determines the regulatory path and the entire competitive set.

  2. 2

    Is there a bank or fintech distribution partner?

    Changes the launch strategy significantly — instant access to existing users (Tikkie model).

  3. 3

    What currency and banking rails?

    SEPA (EU), ACH (US), FPS (UK) — each has different speed, cost, and regulatory requirements.

  4. 4

    Is there a social layer?

    Venmo-style public feed vs. private like Tikkie — a major product philosophy decision.

  5. 5

    Who is the founding network?

    Every successful P2P app started with a dense seed community. Who are PidgePay's first 500 users?
